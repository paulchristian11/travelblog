Etymology

In 1912, a Spanish Jesuit missionary and the curate of Jasaan named Fr. Juan Yras came over the place and renamed Tikala to Claveria in honor of the Spanish Governor-General of the Philippines Don Narciso Claveria y Zaldua.

Demography

In the 2020 census, the population of Claveria, Misamis Oriental, was 52,478 people, with a density of 91 inhabitants per square kilometre or 240 inhabitants per square mile.

History and Administration

Upon the passage of an Act No. 2968, otherwise known as the Artadi Law, it was separated from Bukidnon and was ceded to the province of Misamis Oriental on February 21, 1921, under the administrative control of the Municipal District of Balingasag (MPDC LGU Claveria).

Agriculture

Out of the 150,000 hectares of agricultural land in Misamis Oriental, 17% is in Claveria. This is the same reason why the municipality is considered as one of the food baskets which support the whole province. The major crops are corn, banana, tomato and sub-tropical vegetables..
